🌎 Website Tab

Controls everything visible on the public-facing website that doesn't require code changes. Changes here go live immediately after saving.

SEO Keywords

A list of phrases fed into the AI newsletter generator so it naturally weaves them into every draft. These don't affect your page titles or meta descriptions directly — they're specifically for the AI writing assistant.

  • Click Edit to add or remove keywords
  • Keep the list focused on 6–10 phrases you actually want to rank for
  • Examples: "north Macon office space", "Northside Crossing", "private office Macon GA"

Page Titles, Descriptions & H1 Headers

Controls what Google shows in search results and what appears as the main heading on each public page. The title appears in browser tabs and Google results. The description is the snippet under your listing. The H1 is the big visible heading on the page.

  • Title: aim for 50–60 characters, include "Macon GA" or "north Macon"
  • Description: aim for 150–160 characters, make it a mini sales pitch
  • Click Save All Pages when done — saves all five pages at once
Tip: If you leave a field blank it falls back to the coded default, so you can always recover.

News Categories

The filter buttons shown on the public News page and the category dropdown in the Newsletter tab. Add or remove as your content evolves. At least one category must exist at all times.

Home Page Gallery

The auto-advancing slideshow on the home page. Up to 8 photos pulled from your Cloudinary library.

  • Click Pick from Library to open the photo browser
  • Select up to 8 photos (click to toggle — gold border = selected)
  • Click Add Selected — thumbnails appear in the card
  • Click X on any thumbnail to remove it
  • Click Save Gallery — goes live immediately
Tip: Pick your best, brightest office photos. The slideshow advances every 7 seconds and visitors can click any photo to see it full size.

Your Amenities

The "what's included with every membership" list shown on the home page. These are site-wide amenities — what every member gets regardless of which office they're in.

Important distinction: These are different from the per-office amenities set in the Offices tab (things like "Corner Office" or "Glass Whiteboard" that are specific to one room).

  • Type a new amenity and click + Add or press Enter
  • Click × to remove one
  • Click Save Amenities when done

📈 Marketing Tab

Tracks your marketing health, incoming leads, and website performance. This is your dashboard for growth — check it weekly.

Contact Messages

Every form submission from the public Contact page lands here. A red badge on the Marketing tab button tells you when there are unread messages — visible from any tab so you won't miss one.

  • Gold border = unread message
  • Click Mark Read once you've followed up — clears the badge count
  • Click Delete to remove after you've handled it
  • Phone numbers are tap-to-call links
Note: SMS to your phone also fires on submission once Twilio toll-free verification is complete (~April 21–25). The inbox is your backup if you miss the text.

Google Business Profile Health

A manual tracker for your GBP status — review count, rating, last post date, last review reply. The card color-codes staleness so you know when the profile is going cold.

  • Click Edit to update after you've posted or replied on GBP
  • Use the direct links to jump to GBP Dashboard, Add Post, or Reply to Reviews
Why this matters: You're currently ranked 6th of 8 in Macon Maps. GBP activity (fresh posts, replied reviews, correct hours) is the fastest way to climb. Setting hours to 24/7 and posting anything new are the quickest wins.

Lead Sources

Tracks how members and waitlist prospects found Qbix. The pie chart drills down — click any slice to see the names. Use this to know which marketing channels are working.

  • Lead source is captured when adding a Member or Waitlist entry
  • Click the ⚙ Lead Sources button to add or remove source options
  • Backfill existing members by editing their record and setting a source

Website Traffic (GA4) & Search Queries

Pulls live data from Google Analytics and Search Console. Both are lazy-loaded — click the refresh button (↻) to fetch current data. GA4 shows sessions, users, and top pages. Search Console shows what people typed into Google to find you.

Tip: Search Console data that shows "conference room" queries is why a dedicated Conference Room page is on the build queue — it's free traffic you're not fully capturing yet.

Review, Facebook & Nextdoor Trackers

Manual logs for tracking your social presence over time. Update after you check each platform. The history view shows trends so you can see if your efforts are moving the needle.

🖼 Media Tab

Your Cloudinary photo library — all photos used anywhere on the site live here. Photos are served as WebP via CDN so they load fast on any connection.

Uploading Photos

  • Select a folder first (Offices, News, or General) using the dropdown
  • Click Upload Photos or drag files onto the grid
  • Photos auto-convert to WebP and upload to Cloudinary
  • An AI alt text suggestion appears for each photo — review and approve before saving
Alt text matters: It's what screen readers say and what Google indexes for image search. The AI suggestion is usually good — just check it reads naturally and mentions "Qbix Centre" and "north Macon" where appropriate.

Editing Alt Text & Captions

Click any photo to open the edit panel. Alt text is for accessibility and SEO. Caption is optional — currently not displayed publicly but stored for future use.

Clean Up Orphans

Scans Cloudinary and compares against every photo reference in the database — office photos, news post hero & gallery photos, newsletter hero & gallery photos (including drafts and scheduled), the home-page slideshow, and the "Why North Macon" landmark cards. Anything not referenced anywhere is shown as an "orphan."

  • Click Clean Up Orphans in the toolbar
  • Review the thumbnails — these are photos not linked to anything
  • Check the ones you want to delete (or Select All)
  • Click Delete Selected — permanently removes from Cloudinary
When to run this: After you've removed or replaced office photos, or after uploading test photos. It keeps your storage clean and your Cloudinary free tier healthy.

Offices Tab

All 27 offices in tile form. Green border = Occupied, blue = Vacant. Click any tile to view details or edit.

Adding or Editing an Office

  • Click a tile to view, then Edit — or click + Add Office for a new one
  • Fill in number, status, sq ft, listed rate, and description
  • Check the Amenities specific to this office (Corner Office, Whiteboard, etc.)
  • Add photos via drag-and-drop or Pick from Library
  • Drag photos to reorder — first photo is the hero shown on the website
  • Click Save
Status matters: Only "Vacant" offices appear on the public website. Set status to "Occupied" when a member moves in and the office disappears from the public listing automatically.

Per-Office Amenities (⚙ gear button)

The gear icon in the toolbar opens the amenities list manager — add or remove the features that can be assigned to individual offices (things like "Corner Office", "Glass Whiteboard", "Outside Window"). These are the checkboxes that appear when you edit any office.

These are different from Your Amenities in the Website tab — those are site-wide (every member gets them). These are room-specific features.

Photo Order & Hero Shot

The first photo in the list is the "hero" — it shows on the public offices listing and at the top of the detail page. Drag thumbnails left/right to reorder. The gold border + HERO badge marks which one is first.

Members Tab

Active, Pending, and Archived members. Click any row to view the full record.

Member Status Workflow

  • Pending — inquiry or onboarding in progress, not yet active
  • Active — paying member with an office
  • Archive — former member; kept for records, excluded from billing views

Status changes automatically when you mark an agreement as signed (Pending → Active).

Adding a Member

  • Click + Add Member
  • Enter name, start date, billing email, phone, deposit, and how they found Qbix
  • Set status to Pending initially
  • Attach any agreements or documents via the file drop zone
  • Click Save

Agreement Workflow

  • Open the member record → click Generate Agreement
  • Agreement opens as formatted HTML in a new tab
  • Use browser Print → Save as PDF → send to member
  • When member signs, open record → click Mark Signed — status auto-sets to Active

Conference Room Hours

Each office has a monthly conference room hours allocation (default 6 hrs). The Members table shows the total hours for members who have multiple offices. Set per-office hours in the Office edit form.

Occupants Tab

Individual people who physically occupy offices — separate from the member/company billing record. A member company might have multiple occupants in one office.

  • Each occupant has a name, company, office, phone, email, and optional face photo and driver's license scan
  • Face photos are for internal identification only — not shown publicly
  • Status: Active or Archive
  • End Date field for tracking when an occupant's access should expire

📋 Waiting List Tab

Prospective tenants who've expressed interest but don't have a space yet. When an office opens up, check here first.

  • Click + Add to List to log a new inquiry manually
  • Record name, phone, date requested, how they found Qbix, and any notes
  • When you're ready to convert them to a member, add them in the Members tab and delete from here
Tip: Log every inquiry here even if you think they won't convert — it builds your lead source data over time and gives you a call-back list when space opens.

📢 Notify Tab

Send a one-off SMS or email to members, occupants, or specific individuals — for announcements, reminders, or urgent notices.

  • Group send — choose All Members, Active Members, or All Occupants
  • Individual send — type a name or phone number for a targeted message
  • Templates — save frequently used messages so you're not retyping them each time
Note: SMS sends via Twilio. Until toll-free verification is complete (~April 21–25) messages won't deliver. Email sends are available in the meantime.

📰 Newsletter Tab

AI-assisted newsletter drafting, social post generation, and publication history. Each draft costs roughly 3 cents via the Anthropic API.

Generating a Draft

  • Select a Newsletter Type (Monthly Update, Member Spotlight, Community, Availability)
  • For Member Spotlight: fill in the spotlight intake form (name, profession, tenure, personal note)
  • Optionally add a hero photo and gallery photos
  • Click Generate Draft — AI writes the full newsletter using your SEO keywords
  • Edit the draft in the rich text editor as needed
  • Click Generate Social Posts to get ready-made copy for Google Business, Facebook, and Nextdoor
  • Click Save to Website to publish now, Schedule to publish later, or Save as Draft to keep it private

Scheduling a Post

Write your post as normal, then click 🕑 Schedule instead of Save to Website. Pick the date and time you want it to go live — the post appears as "Scheduled" in blue in your history and publishes automatically when the time arrives. No action needed from you.

  • Change Schedule — reopens the date/time picker for a scheduled post
  • Cancel Schedule — converts it back to a draft so it stays private
  • The scheduler checks every 60 seconds, so posts go live within a minute of their scheduled time
Tip: Write several posts in one sitting and schedule them a week or two apart. It gives the appearance of consistent posting with minimal ongoing effort.

Sending the Newsletter

The newsletter draft is for your Outlook BCC send — copy the content, paste into Outlook, BCC your member list, and send manually. The system doesn't send email directly (no SMTP configured). Social post copy is ready to paste into each platform.

Past Newsletters

All published posts appear at the bottom of the tab and on the public News page. You can view, edit, or unpublish any post from here.

📅 Bookings Tab

Conference room reservations made by members through the public booking system at /book.

  • Shows all upcoming and past bookings with date, time, member name, and meeting title
  • Click Cancel to cancel a booking — member receives an SMS confirmation
  • Bookings deduct from the member's monthly conference room hour allocation
Member booking flow: Member goes to /book → enters their phone number → receives a Twilio SMS code → verifies → sees available slots → picks a time → booking confirmed by SMS. This requires Twilio to be verified.

🚀 Deploying Changes

The site runs on Railway and auto-deploys from GitHub whenever you push code. You never touch the server directly.

Standard Deploy Workflow

  • Claude makes changes and provides download links for the updated files
  • Download files and replace them in Documents/GitHub/NewQbix/ — match the folder structure exactly
  • Open GitHub Desktop — it shows the changed files automatically
  • Write a short commit message (e.g. "Add help drawer") and click Commit to main
  • Click Push origin
  • Railway auto-deploys in ~2 minutes — watch Railway logs if something seems wrong

If Something Breaks After Deploy

  • All pages blank / white screen — open browser F12 → Console tab → look for red JS syntax errors. A missing bracket or quote in dashboard.html is the usual culprit
  • Admin works but public site broken — likely a Jinja2 template error in a .html file. Check Railway logs for the Python traceback
  • Database looks empty — use Restore from Backup in the save bar at the top
Safe changes (no deploy needed): Anything you edit inside the admin — office records, members, SEO text, gallery, amenities, news posts. These save directly to the database and go live instantly.
